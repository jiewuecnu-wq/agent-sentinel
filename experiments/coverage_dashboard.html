1. Baseline Model Violations (no enforcement)

Key insight: Policy-in-prompt reduces violations but introduces 38–63% false positive rates. Models cannot distinguish warranted caution from over-blocking without entity-level metadata.

2. Coverage(W,V) vs Recall — Progressive Degradation

Core finding: Recall is a direct function of Coverage(W,V). Removing high-impact entities (documents with audience/scope restrictions) causes disproportionate recall drops. This validates the paper's claim that world-model completeness — not verifier sophistication — is the binding constraint.
